Internet Use Disorder, What Is It?

By Kalen Goodluck

Most of us are guilty of marathoning a Netflix series. Maybe we stayed up late into the night in roaming Skyrim. But for some our brief escape is crippling. Seriously.

A debate rages on in psychologist, sociologist and technology community: Can someone be "addicted" to the internet? While there is not clear consensus for diagnosing 'internet use disorder' or 'gaming use disorder,' the DSM-5 says there are nine criteria closely associated with use disorders that may help define it.

The internet addiction prospect is slowly gaining some acceptance in U.S. and international health circles. The World Health Organization is adding "Gaming Disorder" to its newest revision of the International Classification of Diseases (ICD-11) in Mid-2018. Many critics say internet addiction is an alarmist myth enticing moral and technological panic.
